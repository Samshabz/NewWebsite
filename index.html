About Me

Hello! My name is Sameer, and I am currently pursuing my Master's degree in Electrical Engineering, focusing on the electric vehicle industry.

My academic journey showcases my dedication, with a 90% average in grade 12, a 70% average during my undergraduate studies, and an 80% mark on my thesis (where I tackled GPS spoofing). I've put these skills to use through various internships and contract positions.

Yet, my ambitions include more than simply my grades. My roles as a class representative, vice-chairman of a community service organization, teaching assistant, and tutor have helped me develop strong leadership and communication skills. These abilities were further enhanced through different projects and an international program, where I was proud to receive the Best Student Award. The blend of these experiences and the skills I've acquired has been crucial for my entrepreneurial efforts and building my network.

As I continue to grow and evolve, I hold onto two core principles: I will only take actions that contribute to my self-improvement and that embody the kind of behavior I wish to see in the world.

E-Design 2

Designed and developed a health monitoring system with a focus on precise circuit design for sensor signal processing.

E-Design 2 Details

This project centered on creating a health monitoring system capable of tracking heart rate, weight, temperature, and steps. It required designing circuits to filter and amplify weak sensor signals while ensuring accuracy through digital processing. The data was displayed on an OLED screen and shared via Wi-Fi.

Through this work, I enhanced my skills in analog design, sensor interfacing, and component research while solving complex problems. The project was assessed via live and recorded demos and a technical report, earning an 86% final mark.

Smart System

Developed a smart RGB lighting strip with interactive patterns, games, and a music visualizer. Tackled challenges like voltage drops and cost-effective power solutions.

Smart System Details

This project showcased my passion for smart home and IoT technologies. I created a 6-meter smart lighting strip with individually addressable RGB LEDs, featuring dynamic music visualization, interactive games, and customizable patterns. The system was controlled through a user-friendly, locally hosted web interface.

Challenges like managing voltage drops and designing efficient power solutions were successfully addressed. Future improvements include advanced power supply designs, modular components, better thermal regulation, and aesthetic enhancements.

I also plan to explore Matter integration to align with future smart home standards.

Analog Amplifier

Designed and built a 24W analog audio amplifier with bass and treble controls, delivering exceptional sound clarity across the frequency spectrum.

Analog Amplifier Detail

As part of a third-year electronics module, I designed and built an amplifier capable of delivering 24W of power to an 8-ohm load. The project involved researching and implementing advanced filtering and high-power amplification techniques, all while using basic analog circuit components.

The amplifier featured precise bass and treble control and was evaluated in a lab setting, where it produced a sound quality that exceeded expectations. The clarity and richness of the sound outperformed high-end commercial systems, filling the lab with powerful and vibrant audio.

This project deepened my understanding of analog circuit design and audio engineering, showcasing the immense potential of foundational components in delivering superior performance.
