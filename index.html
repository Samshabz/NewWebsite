E-Design 1

Designed and developed a multi-input/output emergency lighting system, with a focus on extensive firmware development.

E-Design 1 Details

This project focused on designing hardware and software for a multi-control emergency system powered by batteries. The system featured various analog and digital control mechanisms. A significant portion of the work involved firmware design and testing for integrating various input/output systems and implementing multiple functions, such as SOS and strobe modes.

Through this project, I developed skills in I2C, UART, interrupts, DMA, signal calibration, and enhanced my abilities in report writing, design, and problem-solving. The project was assessed across three phases: automated demos, written theory tests, and a final technical report, where I achieved a 73%. It was a valuable learning experience, and I am proud of the final product.

E-Design 2

Designed and developed a foundational health monitoring system, emphasizing circuit design for accurate sensor signal processing.

E-Design 2 Details

This project involved the development of a health monitoring system capable of measuring heart rate, weight, temperature, and steps. The work focused on designing custom circuits to filter and amplify low-level sensor signals, along with digital processing to ensure accurate measurements. The collected data was displayed on an LCD screen and transmitted via Wi-Fi.

Through this project, I enhanced my skills in analog design, sensor interfacing, component research, and broader problem-solving and design. The assessment comprised live and recorded demonstrations, as well as a final technical report, culminating in a final mark of 86%.
